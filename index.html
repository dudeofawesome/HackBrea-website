What is a hackathon?
Hacking is not about breaking into your school computers. Hackathons are a place where students come together to work on fun projects using technology. It's a great time to hangout with friends, work on something you care about, and have food paid for.
When?
HackBrea will start at 9:00 AM Saturday, May 20th, and continue until 12:00 PM Sunday, May 21st.
Where?
The main event will be in the Wildcat gym, at Brea Olinda High School,
789 Wildcat Way
Brea CA, 92821
There will be other side events taking place througout campus, including learning workshops, gaming tournaments, and more!
Do I need to know how to code?
TeenHacks is for beginners and experts alike. As long as you want to build something (whether you know how to or not), you can come. A main focus of this event is to introduce beginners. Mentors will be available throughout the event to get you started.
What should I bring?
All your friends, computer (laptop or desktop), school ID, phone, toiletries, sleeping bag (optional), and anything else you might need. We will have certain hardware available, but they will be in limited supply. If you want to hack with hardware that we don't have, then you'll need to supply that.
What can I make?
Just about anything. You could make your first website, your first iPhone app, your first Android app, your first robot. The possibilities are endless. Whatever it is, mentors will get you started.
Do I need a team?
Nope. Just come to the event and we will find you a team. We'll be holding a team making session before the hacking begins. You can also find teammates on Facebook beforehand. Working alone is fine too. Teams can have up to 4 people.
What if I don't have an idea?
There will be plenty of other people at HackBrea that will have ideas, or you could join the discussion on Facebook and our subreddit.
Surely an experience like this must be expensive?
America is the land of the free, and HackBrea follows in that great tradition, as it is free too! Meals and snacks are provided the entire event. All you need to worry about is learning and building. Also, like America, we will not be providing travel reimbursement.
Who can attend?
Anyone currently enrolled in high school who wants to learn or improve their skills. If you're in college (or want to help but not hack), you can also sign up to be a mentor. You'll get to help out some newbies and make a great impact on their hackathon experience.
How will I not starve?
We will be providing meals (IN N OUT, Pepe's, IDK what else) and snacks (those gummies and chips 'n stuff) (loads of them!)
Who will own my work?
Don't worry, your team will own the project. We want to see the awesome stuff you can make, but even more than that we want to see your project take off afterward.
How will projects be judged?
You will present your project to a panel of judges, then they will make decisions based on a combination of novelty, utility, design, overall coolness, and technical complexity.
Still have a question?
Feel free to shoot us an email at questions@hackbrea.org
